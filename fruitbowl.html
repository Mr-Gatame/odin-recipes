A.M. Superfoods Bowl


Description

At First Watch, we try not to jump on trendy culinary bandwagons. But after chia seeds started popping up everywhere and in everything, we gave this powerhouse seed a close look and found it fit perfectly with our food mission. Chia seeds contain lots of healthy omega-3 fatty acids, calcium, antioxidants and protein but some people are put off by the texture. That's why we soak the seeds in full-fat coconut milk, giving the dish a creamy-pudding- like appeal. We top it with blackberry preserves, housemade granola and mounds of fresh fruit for the perfect balance

Ingredients:

For Chia Seed Pudding
  • 1/2 cup black chia seeds
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 2 cups coconut milk, canned (culinary coconut milk, not the beverage)
  • 1/4 cup agave nectar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
For Superfoods Bowl
  • 1 slice whole-grain artisan bread
  • 1/4 cup almond butter
  • 1/4 teaspoon Maldon sea salt
  • 3/4 cup chia Seed Pudding (recipe above)
  • 2 tablespoons blackberry preserves
  • 1/2 banana, sliced 1/8 inch thick
  • 2 strawberries, quartered (8 pieces)
  • 1/4 cup blueberries
  • 3/4 cup granola

Directions:

Prepare Chia Seed Pudding
  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine chia seeds and salt.
  2. In a separate mixing bowl, combine coconut milk, agave and vanilla extract and mix with a whisk until all ingredients are incorporated.
  3. Add liquid ingredients to the dry mixture and combine with a whisk until fullt incorporated.
  4. Transfer to a large, airtight container.
  5. Cover and refrigerate for at least 2 hours (or overnight, if possible) before use to allow the chia to "bloom." Stirring may be required after 1 hour.
Finish Superfoods Bowl
  1. Toast one slice of whole-grain artisan bread.
  2. Cut toast corner to corner and spread with almond butter, using the back of a spoon. Sprinkle with Maldon sea salt.
  3. Place a 3/4 cup scoop of Chia Seed Pudding in a bowl.
  4. Add blackberry preserves around the edge of the bowl, on top of the chia seed pudding.
  5. Add sliced bananas, strawberries and blueberries.
  6. Add granola on top of fruit.
  7. Serve with almond-buttered toast on the side.
